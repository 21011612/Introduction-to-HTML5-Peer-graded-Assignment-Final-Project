Introduction

A well-preserved trading port from the 15th–19th centuries, Hội An blends indigenous and international influences in its timber architecture and compact street grid. It was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999.

Learn more at UNESCO — Hoi An Ancient Town .

Things to Try

Top picks (at least three)

  • Walk the Japanese Covered Bridge at dusk.
  • Float a lantern on the Hoài River during the full-moon night.
  • Sample a local bánh mì or cao lầu in the old quarter.
  • Get a tailored outfit made on a short turnaround.

Good to know

  1. Historic core is compact — best explored on foot/bicycle.
  2. Shops often close mid-day; evenings are liveliest by the river.
  3. Full-moon lantern nights draw big crowds — arrive early.
